# Post 6: What building this taught me before writing any code

**Angle:** Honest reflection. The meta-lesson about how to learn.

---

Brevia isn't live yet. I haven't written a single line of production code. But I've learned more from designing it than I expected to.

A few things that stuck with me:

Every decision has a reason, or it should. When I forced myself to write down *why* I made each choice, I caught several decisions I hadn't actually made — I'd just defaulted to something familiar. Documenting first surfaced that.

"Simple" is relative. A URL shortener sounds like a weekend project. It has opinions about character encoding, redirect behavior, expiry logic, database design, and deployment strategy. None of that is complicated, but all of it requires a choice.

Good engineering isn't about knowing the right answer. It's about knowing what question you're answering. 302 vs 301 isn't a right/wrong thing — it depends on whether you care more about performance or analytics. Knowing that is the skill.

I'm building this to grow, not to ship something impressive. If you're early in your career and feel like your projects have to be original or impressive to matter — they don't. They just have to teach you something.

This one's teaching me a lot.
